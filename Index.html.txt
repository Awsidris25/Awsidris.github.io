<section id="about">
  <p class="section-label">About Me</p>
  <h2>Who I Am</h2>
  <div class="divider"></div>
  <div class="about-grid">
    <div class="about-text">
      <p>
        I am a structural engineer and researcher with a PhD in Civil Engineering from Oklahoma State University (2025), where I worked under the supervision of Dr. Mohamed Soliman. My doctoral research focused on the probabilistic performance-based assessment of marine vessels under extreme loading conditions, funded by the Office of Naval Research.
      </p>
      <p>
        I developed novel machine-learning-assisted frameworks for quantifying the fragility, functionality, and long-term resilience of marine vessels. My work introduced new damage state definitions based on low-cycle fatigue analysis, probabilistic demand models, and advanced variance-based sensitivity analyses using high-fidelity finite element simulations.
      </p>
      <p>
        Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master of Science in Structural Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (2019), where I developed a novel finite beam element using the flexibility method to solve nonlinear structural problems with reduced computational cost. I also hold a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from Yarmouk University (2017), where I graduated first among 181 students.
      </p>
      <p>
        I am currently seeking opportunities in academia or research where I can expand my work on performance-based design of marine and civil infrastructure systems, with growing interest in climate resilience and physics-informed machine learning.

<section id="research" style="padding:0;">
<div style="max-width:1100px;margin:auto;padding:90px 2rem 40px;">
  <p class="section-label">Research</p>
  <h2>Research Interests</h2>
  <div class="divider"></div>
</div>
<div class="research-areas">
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>🚢 Performance-Based Design of Marine Vessels</h3>
    <p>Developing integrated probabilistic frameworks for assessing the fragility, functionality, and resilience of ships under extreme hazards such as storms, collisions, and grounding — addressing the limitations of prescriptive design standards.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>🤖 Machine Learning for Structural Engineering</h3>
    <p>Leveraging advanced machine learning algorithms and variance-based sensitivity analysis to build efficient predictive models for structural demand and reliability, reducing reliance on computationally expensive simulations.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>🔩 Finite Element Simulations & Reliability</h3>
    <p>High-fidelity FEM modeling of ship hull girders and steel connections to quantify the effects of geometric imperfections, welding-induced residual stresses, and material nonlinearity on structural reliability.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>🌍 Infrastructure Resilience & Climate Change</h3>
    <p>Investigating the long-term resilience of bridges, vessels, and coastal/offshore structures under climate-induced stressors, including developing physics-informed machine learning models for deterioration prediction.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>⚓ Aluminum Hull Reliability & Corrosion</h3>
    <p>Quantifying the reliability of aluminum ship hulls by accounting for the effects of sensitization and corrosion on material properties and structural performance under monotonic and cyclic loading conditions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>🌊 Probabilistic Hazard & Loss Assessment</h3>
    <p>Extending performance-based earthquake engineering formulations to the marine context, integrating hazard characterization, structural demand modeling, and loss estimation into a unified probabilistic framework.</p>
  </div>
