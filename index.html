About Me

Who I Am

I am a structural engineer and researcher with a PhD in Civil Engineering from Oklahoma State University (2025). My doctoral research focused on the probabilistic performance-based assessment of marine vessels under extreme loading conditions.

I developed novel machine-learning-assisted frameworks for quantifying the fragility, functionality, and long-term resilience of marine vessels. My work introduced new damage state definitions based on low-cycle fatigue analysis, probabilistic demand models, and advanced variance-based sensitivity analyses using high-fidelity finite element simulations.

Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master of Science in Structural Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (2019), where I developed a novel finite beam element using the flexibility method to solve nonlinear structural problems with reduced computational cost. I also hold a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from Yarmouk University (2017), where I graduated first among 181 students.

I am currently seeking opportunities in academia or research where I can expand my work on performance-based design of civil and marine infrastructure systems, with a growing interest in climate resilience and physics-informed machine learning.

Research

Research Interests

🚢 Performance-Based Design of Marine Vessels

Developing integrated probabilistic frameworks for assessing the fragility, functionality, and resilience of ships under extreme hazards such as storms, collisions, and grounding — addressing the limitations of prescriptive design standards.

🤖 Machine Learning for Structural Engineering

Leveraging advanced machine learning algorithms and variance-based sensitivity analysis to build efficient predictive models for structural demand and reliability, reducing reliance on computationally expensive simulations.

🔩 Finite Element Simulations & Reliability

High-fidelity FEM modeling of ship hull girders and steel connections to quantify the effects of geometric imperfections, welding-induced residual stresses, and material nonlinearity on structural reliability.

🌍 Infrastructure Resilience & Climate Change

Investigating the long-term resilience of bridges, vessels, and coastal/offshore structures under climate-induced stressors, including developing physics-informed machine learning models for deterioration prediction.

⚓ Aluminum Hull Reliability & Corrosion

Quantifying the reliability of aluminum ship hulls by accounting for the effects of sensitization and corrosion on material properties and structural performance under monotonic and cyclic loading conditions.

🌊 Probabilistic Hazard & Loss Assessment

Extending performance-based earthquake engineering formulations to the marine context, integrating hazard characterization, structural demand modeling, and loss estimation into a unified probabilistic framework.
